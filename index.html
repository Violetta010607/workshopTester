Hello! I am Beatrice. The following 3 paragraphs are an Introduction of the workshop you are involved in. Please, take some time to read carefully.

In the early stages of my PhD research, before we were all forced to stay at home and then only move within small social groups, I conducted a study with blind people and their guides. Through this study, I have discovered the strong relationships that can develop in sighted guide partnerships. Over time, I have seen how pairs develop a sense of each other’s movements and also use conversation to move fluidly together. It is clear that sighted guide partnerships work very well and rely on people’s shared and creative use of skills and abilities. 

In this follow-up research, I am interested in the ways technology could be used to strengthen this partnership. Unlike other technological solutions that aim to prioritise independence, I am much more curious about how technology can support what already works so well, that is, the way blind people and their guides move together. 

In this workbook, and in each of the following activities, I am inviting pairs to think about the ways they use their bodies together and communicate with one another in subtle and often creative ways. I also introduce some very simple technologies to help stimulate ideas for the role technology might play. What is really important in this workbook is that the activities are understood as starting points for strengthening people’s partnerships and not proposals for final solutions. I would like to create a language and set of tools that we can use to think together.
